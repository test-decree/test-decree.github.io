About

The Department of Defense Electromagnetic and Cyber Research and Experiential Education (DECREE) Virtual Institute is one of the 6 existing Virtual Institutes for the Cyber and Electromagnetic Spectrum Research and Employ (VICEROY) awarded by the Griffiss Institute.

The VICEROY DECREE Virtual Institute is a consortium of 4 universities: Northern Arizona University, University of Houston, University of South Carolina, Northeastern University as the lead to provide a shared curriculum in advanced cyber, cryptography, EMS, strategic foreign languages, and data science. There are also hands-on practical training and research opportunities for the chosen cohort of Northeastern University Undergraduate students and ROTC cadets. Undergraduate Scholars will have the opportunity to take courses and participate in workshops, seminars, and other industry-related events at partner institutions. Additionally, the VICEROY DECREE VI will organize and offer multiple events for scholars and mentors to meet and network with industry and DoD partners and to showcase knowledge, skills, and abilities.

The goal is to increase the pipeline of highly qualified professionals, particularly from historically underserved groups, seeking careers in the DoD and industry partners.

VICEROY DECREE scholars will receive a scholarship of up to $10,000 to participate in the shared curriculum. Scholars will take virtual, credit-bearing coursework from consortium institutions, have access to mentoring programs, as well as access to DoD-related internships, co-ops, and post-graduation employment opportunities. Scholars will be required to complete 8-10 credits within the VICEROY DECREE designated curriculum by the end of the Spring 2024 semester, with at least one-third of credits taken outside their home institution.

Curriculum

DECREE scholars can take courses from a shared virtual institute curriculum. These courses will follow an online instructional method for Visiting VI students. Each scholar needs to complete 8-10 Credits from this multi-institution curriculum (course credits vary from 3 to 4 credit hours; see below). Students must complete at least one semester of full-time study at the Home Institution before participating in the Exchange Program.

Depending on scholars’ background they can select courses which complement their skills and knowledge based on:
Breadth: courses that complement student’s existing background and skills
Depth: courses that amplify and extend student’s existing background and skills

Course curricula is divided into the following areas:

  • Electro-Magnetic Spectrum
  • Cybersecurity
  • Cryptography
  • Data Science
  • Strategic Foreign Language
  • Research
